A little history ….. With   four   decades   developing   downhole   equipment   and   systems   Nigel Halladay   is   in   a   unique   position   of   being   able   to   use   this   collective experience   and   knowledge   for   the   purposes   of   researching,   developing, manufacturing and supplying downhole & related hardware. Graduating   from   the   Camborne   School   of   Mines   and   joining   the   early   stages   of   the CSM   Hot   Dry   rock   [HDR]   Geothermal   Project   known   as   “Hot   Rocks”   granted   an opportunity    to    learn    about    a    wide    field    of    activity    associated    with    the    drilling, stimulation   (explosive   &   hydraulic),   logging   &   circulation   of   a   100 °C,   3km   deep   fluid reservoir.     The     development     of     downhole     instrumentation     was     the     principle responsibility   and   included   a   wide   and   varied   range   of   devices   mostly   aimed   at   150 °C operation, see projects. After   Hot   Rocks   came   a   decade   of   commercially   based   operations   that   grew   from   the HDR   project   taking   the   engineering   technology   to   new   extremes   of   350°C.   The   senior mechanical   design   engineer   &   Engineering   Manager   were   the   key   roles   undertaken during   this   period.   R&D   skills   were   enhanced   by   the   completion   of   a   Master’s   Degree in high temperature seismic receiver design. The   next   decade   and   beyond   saw   the   creation   of   Calidus Engineering,   a   company   specialising   in   the   R&D,   design   and manufacture   of   hostile   environment   tools   taking   “blue   sky” ideas   through   to   finished   products.   As   founder,   Managing Director   and   ultimate   mechanical   design   guru,   Nigel   took the   company   from   start-up   to   35   people   in   a   purpose   built facility, selling on to a Norwegian group in 2012. Halladay’s   Ltd   was   founded   in   2005   as   a   manufacturing   resource,   transferring   the business   to   Calidus   in   2007.   Since   2012   Halladay’s   is   the   vehicle   through   which today’s    consulting    activities    take    place    but    the    name    is    not    new.    The    original company   was   founded   by   W.   E.   Halladay   in   1917   (great   grand   father)   as   a   drop   forge works in Birmingham, sadly closing its doors in 2003.

Collaboration Projects Geothermal Memory Production Logging Tool for 450°C (2016-2017) Halladay’s   were   contracted   by   the   Norwegian,   Oslo   based   research   group   SINTEF,   to   provide   the   mechanical   & thermal   design   input   for   the   development   of   an   extreme   environment   logging   tool   as   part   of   the   EU   funded H2020-DESCRAMBLE      project.   The   tool   was   designed   to   carry   pressure,   temperature   &   casing   collar   locator   (CCL) sensors into a super critical geothermal well environment of 450°C / 450bar for a period in excess of 8 hours. The   project   required   close   cooperation   with   SINTEF   to   integrate   the   electronics   and   sensors   with   the   mechanics while   producing   an   easy   maintain   and   deploy   device.   Regular   meetings   in   both   Oslo   and   via   the   internet   ensured a   timely,   on   budget   design   completion.   Manufacture   was   carried   out   in   Norway   and   the   USA.   R&D   time   included the development of the sealing arrangement and thermal management design. In   December   2017   the   tool   was   successfully   run   in   Italy   to   a   depth   of   2800m   and   a   temperature   of   445°C   while the internal temperature remained below 35°C Well Integrity Evaluation Tool (2017-2020) Working   closely   with   an   oilfield   company   Halladay’s   are   providing   R&D,   design   and   manufacturing   services   for   the production   of   a   well   integrity   tool   that   uses   state   of   the   art   technology   and   techniques.   Drawing   on   nearly      the   full forty    years    of    downhole    tool    design    experience    this    device    makes    extensive    use    of    dynamics,    hydraulics, mechanics   and   electrics   know-how   including   extensive   laboratory   based   test   and   research.   Frequent   face   to   face and   internet   based   meetings,   working   in   close   cooperation   with   the   client   and   others   in   the   development   team has resulted in a very successful first phase and approval to develop the pilot tool prototype.
